Senior Developer Ignores Error Message That Literally Tells Him How To Fix The Problem, Spends Days Making Random Guesses

SAN FRANCISCO - In a stunning display of incompetence and stubbornness, principal developer Jason Lee reportedly ignored an error message that literally told him how to fix the problem he was facing, and instead spent days making random guesses and blaming other factors. According to sources close to Lee, the error message clearly stated that he needed to update his dependencies and run a migration script, but he refused to read it or follow its instructions, preferring to try various hacks and workarounds that only made the situation worse. “I don’t need some stupid error message to tell me what to do,” Lee said, as he deleted and reinstalled his entire project for the fifth time. “I know what I’m doing. It’s probably a bug in the framework or something. Or maybe the network is slow. Or maybe it’s the weather. Yeah, that’s it. The weather is messing with my code.” Lee’s colleagues expressed frustration and disbelief at his behavior, saying that they had tried to help him but he rejected their advice and insisted on doing things his own way. “He’s wasting so much time and energy on something that could be solved in minutes if he just read the damn error message,” one coworker said. “It’s like he has a mental block or something. He thinks he’s too smart to need any help, but he’s actually making himself look like an idiot.” At press time, Lee was still struggling with the same problem, while the error message patiently waited for him to pay attention to it.
